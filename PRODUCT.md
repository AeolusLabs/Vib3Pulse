## Users

Three distinct user types sharing one app.

**Social Users (18-35, London + Lagos diaspora):** Going out is their identity. They want to find what's happening tonight, coordinate with their crew, buy tickets without faff, and get home safely. They're on mobile, often in low-light environments, moving quickly. Discovery is emotional, not transactional.

**Event Organisers (DJs, promoters, brands):** Running an event is a small business. They need to create events, sell tickets, track RSVPs, and get paid reliably. They care about reach (how many people see my event) and trust (will tickets actually work at the door).

**Venue Operators (club/bar owners):** They care about occupancy, reputation, and safety. Vib3Pulse gives them a presence online and a connection to promoters. They are less frequent users but high-value ones.

## Product Purpose

Vib3Pulse consolidates nightlife discovery, ticketing, social coordination, and personal safety into a single platform — solving fragmentation across Eventbrite, WhatsApp, Instagram, and no safety tool at all. First market: London. Secondary: Lagos. The core differentiator is the safety system (Safety Buddy, SOS alerts, check-in timers), which makes Vib3Pulse more than a ticketing app — it's a trusted companion for a night out. Success: users return every weekend, and organisers choose Vib3Pulse over Eventbrite because the audience is already there.

## Brand Personality

Bold, trustworthy, electric.

The brand is confident without being loud, editorial without being cold, and safety-conscious without being clinical. It feels like a publication that happens to sell tickets — think Resident Advisor's editorial authority, Boiler Room's cultural credibility, and Robinhood's design restraint. The name is stylised as "Vib3Pulse" (with a 3).

## Anti-references

- **Eventbrite / Facebook Events**: corporate, transactional, zero personality
- **Generic SaaS purple-gradient**: the purple-to-pink gradient every "modern" app uses — not unique, not premium
- **Neon cyberpunk**: overdesigned, inaccessible, screams "gaming app"
- **Soft pastels / neobrutalism**: wrong demographic, wrong energy
- **Dashboard-as-hero**: stat grids, metric cards — not a business tool landing page

## Design Principles

1. **Editorial confidence.** Treat every surface like a publication. Typography does the heavy lifting. Whitespace is not waste — it is the product.
2. **Single accent, total commitment.** One brand color (electric violet) used deliberately. Not gradients. Not rainbows. The restraint makes the accent mean something.
3. **Safety without alarm.** The safety system should feel calm and trustworthy, not clinical. Use the same visual language but softer — never red-and-warning by default.
4. **Speed implies trust.** Fast load, instant feedback, no loading spinners where none are needed. Organisers and social users both interpret sluggishness as unreliability.
5. **Mobile-first is non-negotiable.** The app wraps into iOS/Android via Median. Every decision should be tested at 375px first.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum for all surfaces. AAA preferred for critical flows (auth, safety).
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: respect it. Animations are enhancement, not content.
- Colour is never the only signal: icons, labels, and text always accompany colour indicators.
- Touch targets: minimum 44×44px on all interactive elements.
